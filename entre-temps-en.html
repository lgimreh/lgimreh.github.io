Heritage rehabilitation

Fall 2020

Heritage rehabilitation project at St-Michael's anglican church and communal hall. The project is mindful of the site's history in its combination of references to Christian tradition with the architecture of contemporary meditation spaces in order to create a resourcing space for Sillery's community.

In line with the respect of this historic building, the biggest modifications to the appearance of the complex are limited to the addition of corridors and slight modifications to the space plan.

Concept diagram

Perspective section view

Site plan + ground floor plan

The building's interior, as shown by the diagram above, has both individual and collective meditation spaces. These functions include a gym for group sessions, a massage therapy center in the basement and an area for socializing within the church building, which would still need to be used by the parishioners on certain days.

On the outside, there's a large garden divided in three main sensory sections that are connected by a looping track that can be used for pacing around the premises. The garden has a section with lavender, another with white roses and a third section with rows of trees that will cast shadows on group classes.
